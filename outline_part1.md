### Ch 1: The Brine-Touch

- **POV:** Cass Bellwright, third-person limited
- **Location:** The Pens, Last Harbor
- **Save the Cat beat:** Opening Image + Setup (first half)
- **% mark:** 0-4%
- **Emotional arc:** Competent control → quiet grief
- **Try-fail cycle:** Yes-but (transfer succeeds, but the cost is visible)

**Beats:**
1. Cass transfers a memory for a wealthy merchant—a childhood birthday. She soaks her hands in brine, presses them against the leatherback's skin, speaks the memory aloud. The creature's skin darkens with the pattern. The merchant pays in salt-crystals.
2. After the merchant leaves, Cass touches her own left temple. Something's missing now. She can't remember what she had for breakfast. The random cost has taken a small, recent memory.
3. Cass walks through the Pens afterward, greeting the mantas by name. She stops at Quiet—the manta that carries Elara's laugh. She presses her forehead to the creature's cool skin. She can almost hear it.
4. A Deep-Priest attendant approaches. The Council has summoned her. They have a commission.

**Plants:**
- Cass touches her left temple when she's trying to remember (physical tell)
- Quiet the manta is introduced—specific, named, carrying something precious
- The Council summons is mentioned but not explained

**Payoffs:** None (first chapter)

**Character movement:** We see Cass's competence and her wound in the same scene. She is good at her work. She is not good at letting go.

**The lie reinforced:** Cass believes she can preserve Elara through memory transfer. The chapter shows her using the gift successfully—reinforcing that the lie works.

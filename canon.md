- The Salt-Scar's western coast was once lined with thriving port cities; now those cities sit miles inland. (world.md)

- The ground on the Salt-Scar is a crust of crystallized minerals that cracks like thin ice. (world.md)
- When wind blows across the salt flats, it produces a high-pitched whistle as it passes over crystal formations. (world.md)
- At night, the salt flats glow faintly with residual bioluminescence. (world.md)
- The salt flats are not dead; the crystalline crust grows, cracks, and reforms. (world.md)
- The Cartographer's Guild has recorded patterns in the salt flats that mirror the bioluminescent patterns in the ocean. (world.md)
- The land is learning to speak. (world.md)

- The God-Step cliffs rise 800 feet sheer from the water. (world.md)

## Magic System Rules

### Sea-Wrighting: The Transfer of Memory

**The Medium Requirement**
- Memories can only be transferred onto the living skin of deep-sea creatures—specifically those that migrate to abyssal depths. (world.md)
- Leatherback manta-rays are preferred because their skin holds the memory longest (up to three months). (world.md)
- Jellyfish hold memories for days. (world.md)
- Scale-fish hold memories for hours. (world.md)
- The creature must be alive during the transfer for the memory to reach the deeps. (world.md)
- The creature must remain alive for the memory to reach the deeps. (world.md)

**The Transfer Gesture**
- The sea-wright soaks both hands in brine. (world.md)
- The brine must be ocean water, not salt-flat brine. (world.md)
- The water must be living water that has touched the deeps within the past tide cycle. (world.md)
- The sea-wright presses their palms flat against the creature's skin. (world.md)
- The sea-wright wills the memory outward. (world.md)
- The memory leaves the sea-wright's mind as a physical sensation: a pulling behind the eyes, a taste of copper, a sound like paper tearing. (world.md)

**The Limitation of Surface Area**
- A single manta-ray can hold approximately three distinct memories before its skin is saturated. (world.md)
- Overloading a manta-ray causes the creature to go into shock and die. (world.md)
- If the creature dies from overloading, the memories are lost entirely. (world.md)
- A sea-wright can work with multiple creatures, but each transfer costs the same. (world.md)

**The Cost (Permanent)**
- Each memory transferred leaves a scar on the sea-wright's mind. (world.md)
- The scar is not a hole—it is an erasure. (world.md)
- The sea-wright does not forget that they forgot. (world.md)
- The sea-wright remembers having known something, but the content is gone. (world.md)
- This creates a distinctive psychological wound: the hollow knows the shape of what they lost without being able to fill it. (world.md)

**The Cost (Variable)**
- The more emotionally charged the memory, the larger the scar. (world.md)
- A memory of what you ate for breakfast costs a minor fact (the capital of a neighboring country, the name of a childhood friend). (world.md)
- A memory of your child's face costs a major life-skill (how to read, how to tie knots, the ability to distinguish colors). (world.md)
- The sea-wright does not choose what is taken; the sea chooses. (world.md)

**The Return**
- When a creature carrying a memory reaches the abyssal plains, the old gods "read" the memory. (world.md)
- The creature then begins a slow migration back to shallower waters. (world.md)
- If the creature returns to the surface within the memory's duration (three months for manta-rays), the sea-wright can receive the memory back by pressing their palms to the creature again. (world.md)
- The cost is not refunded when a memory is returned. (world.md)
- The scar remains even after the memory is returned. (world.md)
- The memory, if returned, is thinner—like a copy of a copy. (world.md)

**The Limitation of Purpose**
- The old gods do not answer; they only read. (world.md)
- No sea-wright has ever received a direct response from the deeps. (world.md)
- The Church of the Last Wave claims the gods are waiting for the right question. (world.md)
- The Deep-Sailors say the gods do not know how to speak in human terms—they only arrange, and humans are not smart enough to read the arrangement. (world.md)

**Additional Rules**
- A sea-wright cannot transfer a false memory; the sea reads the truth of the memory and rejects anything fabricated. (world.md)
- Attempting to transfer a fabricated memory causes the sea-wright to lose the real memory that the fabrication was based on. (world.md)
- A sea-wright cannot transfer a memory on behalf of someone else without taking the cost themselves; the scar is always personal. (world.md)
- The old gods do not intervene; they read, they arrange, they do not answer questions, grant wishes, or perform miracles. (world.md)
- Any claim otherwise is either superstition or fraud. (world.md)

### The Forgetting Plague
- The Forgetting Plague is a recent phenomenon. (world.md)
- Some hollows are developing a contagious form of memory loss that spreads through physical contact. (world.md)
- The Church calls the Forgetting Plague divine punishment. (world.md)

### Unexplained Magic Phenomena
- Some memories transferred into manta-rays do not reach the deeps; they vanish en route. (world.md)
- The creatures return to the surface with blank skin, as if the memory was intercepted. (world.md)
- No one knows what is taking the memories. (world.md)

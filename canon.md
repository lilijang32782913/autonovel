## Geography

- The Salt-Scar smells of brine and something metallic — old blood, old iron. (world.md)

- The Salt-Scar speaks when the wind blows across the crystals, vibrating at specific frequencies. (world.md)

- The Salt-Scar does not support life. (world.md)
- The Salt-Scar is expanding faster than cartographers can map it. (world.md)

## Magic System Rules

### Sea-Wrighting: The Transfer
- Sea-wrighting is the transfer of human memory onto the skin of deep-sea creatures through direct physical contact in salt water. (world.md)
- The sea-wright must submerge both hands in brine for no less than three minutes. (world.md)
- The brine must be ocean water, not artificial salt water. (world.md)
- The water must be alive — taken from the retreating ocean within the past seven days. (world.md)
- The sea-wright presses both palms against the living flesh of a deep-sea creature. (world.md)
- Leatherback manta-rays are preferred for sea-wrighting because their skin is thick enough to hold a full memory. (world.md)
- Smaller creatures can only carry fragments of memories. (world.md)
- The sea-wright concentrates on a single memory during the transfer. (world.md)
- The memory travels down the arms, through the palms, into the creature's skin. (world.md)
- The transfer process takes between thirty seconds and five minutes, depending on the memory's weight. (world.md)
- The creature carries the memory into the deeps. (world.md)
- The memory is read by the old gods beneath the sea-floor. (world.md)
- The sea-wright cannot know what the gods do with the memory. (world.md)

### Sea-Wrighting: The Cost
- Every memory transferred leaves a permanent scar on the sea-wright's mind. (world.md)
- The scar takes the form of a specific loss: one day of experience, one face, one skill, one emotion. (world.md)
- The sea-wright does not choose what is lost; the memory trades itself. (world.md)
- Multiple transfers accelerate the loss. (world.md)
- A sea-wright who performs three transfers in a single day will lose weeks of memory. (world.md)
- The record is seven transfers in one day. (world.md)
- The sea-wright who performed seven transfers in one day, Reska of the Drowned Coast, forgot her entire life up to age twenty-two. (world.md)
- Reska of the Drowned Coast is now sixty and believes she is still a child. (world.md)
- The memory loss is permanent; no magic, medicine, or prayer can restore a memory given to the deeps. (world.md)
- The sea-wright's mind heals around the hole, but the hole remains. (world.md)
- The sea-wright cannot transfer the same memory twice. (world.md)
- Once a memory is given, it is gone from the mind entirely. (world.md)
- The sea-wright will not remember having remembered the transferred memory. (world.md)

### Sea-Wrighting: Limitations
- The creature must be alive during the transfer; dead flesh cannot hold memory. (world.md)
- The creature must be returned to the ocean within one hour, or the memory degrades into meaningless noise. (world.md)
- A single creature can carry multiple memories, but each additional memory increases the chance the creature will be attacked by predators. (world.md)
- The old gods hunger for memories and will kill to consume them. (world.md)
- Sea-wrighting does not work on humans. (world.md)
- The transfer requires a creature that lives in the deeps and can survive the pressure where the old gods dwell. (world.md)
- The brine used must be from the retreating ocean; water from the salt flats (re-dissolved salt) does not work. (world.md)
- The ocean is alive; the flats are dead. (world.md)
- Sea-wrighting cannot be reversed; once a memory is given, it is gone. (world.md)
- There is no secret technique or loophole; the cost is absolute. (world.md)

### The Memory-Share (Reverse Transfer)
- Among sea-wrights, it is customary to offer a memory to a dying colleague. (world.md)
- The memory is pressed into the dying person's skin (not a creature). (world.md)
- The transfer works on humans in reverse, from giver to receiver, but only when the receiver is near death. (world.md)
- The dying person experiences the memory as their own. (world.md)
- The dying person dies with one more moment of life than they would have had. (world.md)

## Cultural

### Customs
- **The Salt-Greeting:** When two people meet on the Salt-Scar, they touch their tongues to their own palms to prove they are not dehydrated. (world.md)
- **The Memory-Share:** Among sea-wrights, it is customary to offer a memory to a dying colleague by pressing it into the dying person's skin. (world.md)
- **The Hollow's Feast:** Once a year, the hollows are brought out of their compounds and fed a feast with the best food and wine, sung to, and thanked. (world.md)
- The Hollow's Feast is a celebration and a mourning — a goodbye to the people the hollows were. (world.md)

### Taboos
- Never ask a sea-wright what they have forgotten; it is the deepest insult. (world.md)
- Never point at the ocean at night; the patterns are watching, and the ocean does not like being demanded of. (world.md)
- Never eat salt from the Salt-Scar; the crystals hold old memories and will give you someone else's nightmares. (world.md)

### Festivals
- **The Night of the Glow:** On the longest night of the year, the algae bloom brightest; coastal cities hold festivals on the shore; people sing to the ocean; it is the only night when the sea-wrights do not work. (world.md)
- **The Day of the Drowned:** A day of mourning for everyone who has died in the retreating ocean; families walk to the edge of the water and leave offerings — food, flowers, letters weighted with stones. (world.md)
- On the Day of the Drowned, the offerings are consumed by the deep-sea creatures and the memories are carried down. (world.md)

### Food
- **Salt-bread:** Flatbread made with flour and re-dissolved Salt-Scar salt; it is dense, chewy, and tastes like tears. (world.md)
- **Ray-meat:** The flesh of manta-rays that died naturally; it is stringy and bland and the only reliable source of protein. (world.md)
- **Algae-soup:** Made from the bioluminescent algae; it glows faintly in the bowl; it tastes of copper and honey; it is considered a delicacy; it is mildly hallucinogenic. (world.md)
- Eaters of algae-soup report seeing patterns behind their eyelids. (world.md)
- The retreating ocean has made fishing unreliable. (world.md)

### Clothing
- **Scar-boots:** Heavy boots with metal cleats and ankle braces, necessary for walking on the salt flats. (world.md)
- **Memory-cloaks:** Cloaks made from the cured skin of manta-rays; they are waterproof, warm, and carry the faint ghost of the memory the ray once held; wearing one is a status symbol. (world.md)
- **Salt-masks:** Worn by miners on the Salt-Scar; they cover the mouth and nose, filtering out the fine salt dust that would otherwise crystallize in the lungs. (world.md)

### Coming of Age
- At sixteen, a child from a coastal city walks to the edge of the ocean alone and spends one night on the shore watching the patterns. (world.md)
- If the child can describe the patterns the next morning, they are considered an adult. (world.md)
- If the child cannot describe the patterns — if the ocean did not speak to them — they try again the next year. (world.md)
- Sea-wright children undergo a different ritual: at twelve, they press their hands into the water for the first time. (world.md)
- If the brine accepts a sea-wright child (if the transfer begins without conscious effort), they are marked as potential sea-wrights. (world.md)
- If the brine does not accept a sea-wright child, they are apprenticed to another trade. (world.md)

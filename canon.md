## Magic System Rules

- Sea-Wrights can temporarily transfer human memories onto the skin of leatherback manta-rays and other deep-sea creatures. (seed.txt)
- To transfer a memory, the Sea-Wright soaks both hands in brine (salt water mixed with a drop of their own blood) for exactly three breaths. (world.md)
- The Sea-Wright presses their palms against the skin of a leatherback manta-ray (or other approved deep-sea creature). (world.md)
- The Sea-Wright speaks the memory aloud during transfer. (world.md)
- The memory leaves the Sea-Wright's mind and enters the creature's skin as a visible pattern: a dark bruise, a swirl of pigment, a scar-like line. (world.md)
- The transfer takes as long as the memory took to live. (world.md)
- One hour of memory takes one hour to transfer. (world.md)
- The Sea-Wright permanently loses the memory they transferred. (world.md)
- The Sea-Wright cannot recall, dream, or recognize the transferred memory if described to them. (world.md)
- The Sea-Wright loses one other memory of similar emotional weight, chosen at random from their mind. (world.md)
- The random loss cannot be predicted or controlled. (world.md)
- A Sea-Wright who transfers a childhood memory may also lose the face of their spouse. (world.md)
- The loss is permanent. (world.md)
- No known magic can restore a transferred memory. (world.md)
- Only emotional memories can be transferred. (world.md)
- Factual memories (numbers, routes, recipes) resist transfer and often fail. (world.md)
- The creature must be alive and conscious during transfer. (world.md)
- Unconscious or dead creatures reject the memory. (world.md)
- A single creature can carry up to seven memories before the skin tears and the memories are lost forever. (world.md)
- Memories degrade if the creature dies. (world.md)
- A dying creature's carried memories become fragmented and unreliable. (world.md)
- Sea-Wrights cannot transfer their own memories to another human. (world.md)
- Sea-Wrights can only transfer memories to sea-creatures. (world.md)
- The creature carries the memory to the deeps, where the Sleepers (or the Mother Sea) "read" it. (world.md)
- What the Sleepers do with the memories is unknown. (world.md)
- Some Deep-Priests claim the Sleepers feed on the memories. (world.md)
- Others say the memories are added to the Mother Sea's own mind. (world.md)
- Memories that are not read eventually fade from the creature's skin after approximately one year. (world.md)
- Attempting to transfer a memory without brine causes the memory to fragment. (world.md)
- The Sea-Wright loses the fragmented memory, but the creature receives only noise—scrambled images, emotions without context. (world.md)
- "Broken memories" sometimes drive creatures mad. (world.md)
- Transferring a memory to a creature that already carries seven causes the oldest memory to be ejected and lost forever. (world.md)
- Forcing a transfer on a chained or captive creature produces "hollow memories"—the pattern appears, but the emotional weight is missing. (world.md)
- The Sleepers reject hollow memories. (world.md)
- Stage 1 of Hollowing (1-10 transfers): Occasional blank moments, forgetting why you entered a room, misplacing familiar names. (world.md)
- Stage 2 of Hollowing (10-30 transfers): Losing significant life events, recognizing your spouse but not your wedding day, knowing you have a child but not their birth. (world.md)
- Stage 3 of Hollowing (30-50 transfers): Forgetting faces, forgetting skills. (world.md)
- Stage 4 of Hollowing (50+ transfers): Hollow—no new long-term memories form, the Sea-Wright lives in a perpetual present, unable to learn, love, or plan. (world.md)
- Some memories transferred to creatures return as dreams to the original owner. (world.md)
- This return is rare and considered a sign of the Mother Sea's favor. (world.md)
- Creatures that carry memories of drowning sometimes beach themselves, as if trying to return the memory. (world.md)
- There is one recorded case of a creature carrying a memory for 40 years without degradation. (world.md)
- That creature was never caught. (world.md)
- That creature's name in Deep-Priest records is "The Keeper." (world.md)

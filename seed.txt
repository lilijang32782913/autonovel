THE LAST SKETCH OF THE SEA-WRIGHT

HOOK: A cartographer who can draw memories onto the skin of dying sea-creatures is summoned to map the final coast of a shrinking ocean—and finds the ocean is fleeing on purpose.
WORLD: The Salt-Scar is a continent where the sea has been retreating for generations, leaving behind a vast, crystalline salt flat that crackles underfoot and tastes of ancient grief. Port cities now sit miles inland, their harbors filled with petrified ships. The remaining ocean glows faintly at night with bioluminescent algae that form patterns—the ocean’s own language. Each wave is a syllable.
MAGIC/COST: Sea-Wrights can temporarily transfer human memories onto the skin of leatherback manta-rays and other deep-sea creatures by soaking their hands in brine and pressing them against the animal’s flesh. The creature then carries that memory to the deeps—where it is read by the old gods beneath the sea-floor. But each memory transferred leaves a permanent scar on the sea-wright’s own mind: a day lost, a loved one’s face forgotten, a skill erased. The most powerful sea-wrights are hollow, amnesiac shells.
TENSION: Personal: The cartographer, Ninsar, is losing her memories of her daughter, who drowned years ago. She takes the commission hoping to preserve her daughter’s face by drawing it onto a manta-ray. Cosmic: The ocean’s retreat reveals the sleep of an ancient being whose awakening will crack the world in half. Ninsar must choose between saving the memory of her child and warning the world.
THEME: Is it better to remember a beautiful lie or to forget in order to act for the living?
WHY IT'S NOT GENERIC: The magic system is intimate and somatic, tied to memory loss as a direct cost, and the setting—a shrinking, sentient ocean and its crystalline aftermath—feels like nothing you’ve read before. Also, no swords.

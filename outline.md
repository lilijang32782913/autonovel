## Ch 1: The Last Harbor

- **POV:** Ninsar, third-person limited
- **Location:** Salt-Scar coast, the abandoned port of Last Harbor
- **Save the Cat beat:** Opening Image + Setup (first half)
- **% mark:** 0-4%
- **Emotional arc:** Professional competence → Unease at what she's seeing
- **Try-fail cycle:** No-but (she completes the job but something is wrong)

**Beats:**
1. **Opening Image:** Ninsar kneels on a salt-encrusted dock, hands submerged in a brine bucket. Before her, a dying manta-ray gasps on the planks. She draws a merchant's memory of his wedding day onto the creature's skin—the bride's face, the salt-crusted cake, the way the wind caught her veil. The manta's skin ripples, accepting the transfer. Ninsar feels the memory leave her: the taste of her own wedding meal, gone. She doesn't flinch.
2. **The cost made visible:** The merchant weeps with joy as his wife's face appears on the manta's flank. Ninsar touches her left temple, searching for the lost taste. Nothing. She packs her tools. The merchant offers her more coin. She takes it without thanks.
3. **The ocean's behavior:** As Ninsar walks the empty harbor, she notices the bioluminescent algae forming patterns she's never seen—not the usual spiral of greeting, but something jagged. Urgent. She reads it: *Flee. Flee. Flee.* She dismisses it as fatigue.
4. **The commission arrives:** A Corda messenger finds her at the inn. Sealed letter, blue wax, the ship-in-dry-dock crest. Maret Corda requests her presence in Cantamura. "A final commission. The last coast. Name your price."
5. **The piece of sea-glass:** Ninsar takes out the sea-glass Elani gave her. She rubs it between thumb and forefinger. She cannot remember the day Elani gave it to her. She traded that memory years ago.

**Plants:**
- The jagged bioluminescent pattern (the ocean's warning language)
- The piece of sea-glass (Elani's last gift, Ninsar's touchstone)
- The manta's acceptance of the memory transfer (establishes mechanics)
- The lost taste of her wedding meal (first concrete example of cost)

**Payoffs:** None yet (first chapter)

**Character movement:** Ninsar is introduced as competent, hollow, driven. She completes the job but feels the wrongness of the ocean's patterns. She takes the Corda commission because she needs the money—and because she knows the great leatherback manta is said to be in the deep waters near the final coast.

**The Lie reinforced:** *If I can preserve enough of Elani's memory, she will not truly be dead.* — Ninsar tells herself this commission is about money. The reader sees it's about the manta.

**~Word count target:** 3,500

## Ch 2: The Salt Road

- **POV:** Ninsar
- **Location:** The salt flats between Last Harbor and Cantamura
- **Save the Cat beat:** Setup (second half) + Theme Stated
- **% mark:** 4-8%
- **Emotional arc:** Resolve → Dread → Curiosity
- **Try-fail cycle:** Yes-but (she makes progress toward Cantamura but encounters something that shakes her)

**Beats:**
1. **Journey across the flats:** Ninsar travels by salt-skiff—a boat with runners, pulled by a team of salt-hounds. The crystalline flats crackle under the runners. She passes petrified ships, their hulls encrusted with salt, their masts pointing at nothing. The silence is absolute except for the crackling.
2. **Encounter with a salt-flat settler:** A woman emerges from a shack built from shipwreck timbers. She offers water. Ninsar accepts. The woman's hands are cracked, her eyes wild. She asks if Ninsar is a sea-wright. When Ninsar says yes, the woman spits. "You're the reason it's leaving. You and your kind, taking memories, feeding the deep. The ocean is afraid of what you've woken."
3. **Theme Stated:** The woman's daughter, a girl of about eight, hides behind her mother's skirts. She stares at Ninsar's hands. "Mama says you steal memories," the girl says. "Is that true?" Ninsar doesn't answer. The girl continues: "Mama says the ocean is running away because something underneath is waking up. Is that true?" Ninsar says: "The ocean doesn't run. It's water. It moves." The girl: "Things that move can run."
4. **The ocean speaks again:** That night, camped on the flats, Ninsar watches the bioluminescent algae in a shallow pool. The patterns are different now—not jagged, but rhythmic. A pulse. A heartbeat. She realizes: the ocean is not just speaking. It's breathing.
5. **Decision:** Ninsar decides to push through to Cantamura without rest. The commission matters. The manta matters. Everything else is distraction.

**Plants:**
- The salt-flat woman's accusation (the ocean is afraid of something woken)
- The girl's question: "Is that true?" (echoes throughout)
- The ocean's breathing rhythm (establishes it as living)
- The petrified ships (visual of what the retreat leaves behind)

**Payoffs:** None yet

**Character movement:** Ninsar's certainty is scratched. The woman's accusation lands—she has no answer for the girl. She doubles down on the commission as a way to avoid the question.

**The Lie challenged:** The girl asks if Ninsar steals memories. Ninsar has no good answer. The Lie requires her to believe she's preserving, not stealing. The question lingers.

## Ch 3: The Academy of Dead Water

Ninsar is searched at the gate. Her brine-knife is confiscated. "Academy policy," the guard says. "No sea-wright tools inside."
2. **Meeting with Rector Suvaine:** Suvaine's office is lined with preserved sea-creatures in jars. She offers Ninsar tea. Ninsar refuses. Suvaine explains that the Academy has been monitoring the ocean's retreat. "It's accelerating," she says. "We need a final map before it's gone. House Corda has funded the expedition. You will report to Maret Corda directly."
3. **The archive:** Suvaine shows Ninsar the Academy's collection—thousands of memory-sketches preserved on dried manta-hide. "Every sea-wright who has ever worked for us," Suvaine says. **The Lie challenged:** The Academy's archives are full of preserved memories—but the people who gave them are hollow. Ninsar sees herself in them. She does not want to.

## Ch 4: The Corda Contract

The walls are covered in maps of the Salt-Scar, each one showing the ocean's retreat over generations. The oldest maps show a sea that covered half the continent. The newest shows a thin blue line at the edge.
2. **Maret Corda:** She is taller than Ninsar expected, severe, dressed in deep blue wool. She does not offer tea. She does not offer a seat. "You are the best," Maret says. "I have no interest in the second-best. The final coast must be mapped before the ocean disappears entirely. You will have three months, a crew of twelve, and unlimited resources. In exchange, you will give House Corda exclusive rights to the map."

"The ocean is retreating into these trenches," Maret says. "We need to know what's down there. Before it's gone." Ninsar: "You want me to map the bottom of the world." It carries memories directly to the deep gods. If you have something you wish to preserve—something that cannot be lost—the leatherback can carry it where nothing else can reach."
5. **The deal:** Ninsar accepts. She does not ask how Maret knows about Elani. She does not want to know.

**Plants:**
- The deep trenches (the ocean's hiding place)

- The oldest maps showing a vast sea (the scale of loss)

**The Lie reinforced:** Ninsar accepts the contract because it gives her the leatherback. She tells herself the map is incidental. The real work is preserving Elani. The Lie is stronger than ever.
